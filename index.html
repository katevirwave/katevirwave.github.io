The one idea I keep building from

My TEDxUCL thesis, in one breath.

Modern systems are designed for a fictional human — rational, consistent, emotionally stable, cognitively available on demand.

That human does not exist.

When real people — stressed, neurodivergent, overwhelmed — interact with these systems, their predictable responses are called failure.

I build toward a different assumption.

— from The Myth of CALM, TEDx
